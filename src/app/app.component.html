Neither Sphere nor Cube - Analyzing the Particle Shape using Small-Angle Scattering and the Superball Model

Abstract

Accurate characterization of the nanocrystal shape with high statistical relevance is essentialfor exploiting the strongly shape-dependent properties of cuboidal nanoparticles towards applica-tions. This work presents the development of a new small-angle scattering form factor based onthe superball geometry. The superball quantifies the characteristic rounding of corners and edgesof cuboidal nanoparticles with a single parameter. Applied to small-angle scattering data of suffi-ciently monodisperse nanoparticles, the superball form factor enables differentiation between theeffects of extended particle size distribution and irregular particle shape. The quantitative applica-tion of the superball form factor is validated against microscopy data for a series of monodispersenanoparticles and implemented into the user-friendly, open source software Sasview.
